# Ecommerce-sales-funnel-analysis-sql
SQL project analyzing customer conversion funnels, marketing channel performance, revenue metrics, and customer purchase journeys using BigQuery.

## Overview
This project analyzes customer behavior in an e-commerce business using Google BigQuery SQL. The objective is to evaluate customer conversion across the sales funnel, compare marketing channel performance, analyze customer purchase journeys, and calculate revenue KPIs to support data-driven business decisions.

## Business Problem
The company wants to identify where customers drop off in the purchasing journey, determine which marketing channels generate the highest-quality traffic, and measure revenue performance to improve conversion rates and profitability.

## Results
A total of 5,000 website visitors generated 826 purchases, resulting in an overall conversion rate of 16.52%. 

The largest customer drop-off occurred between the Page View and Add-to-Cart stages, where only 31.06% of visitors added products to their carts, highlighting the need to optimize product pages. 

Once customers added products to their carts, conversion remained strong, with 91.88% of users completing their purchase after reaching the payment stage. 

Marketing channel analysis showed that Email Marketing achieved the highest overall conversion rate (34%), while Organic Search generated the highest traffic and total number of purchases. Paid Advertising delivered the strongest Cart-to-Purchase conversion rate (57%), indicating highly qualified traffic, whereas Social Media generated high traffic but the lowest purchase conversion (7%).

Revenue analysis revealed total sales of $87,975.11, with an Average Order Value (AOV) of $106.51 and Revenue per Visitor of $17.60. 

Customer journey analysis showed that converted users completed their purchase in an average of 24.63 minutes, spending approximately 11.16 minutes from product view to cart and 13.47 minutes from cart to purchase. 

## Business Recommendations
The business should optimize product pages by improving product descriptions, images, reviews, and calls-to-action to increase the low 31.06% view-to-cart conversion rate and reduce customer drop-off at the top of the funnel.

Marketing investment should prioritize email campaigns, organic search optimization, and high-performing paid advertising, as these channels demonstrated stronger conversion rates and higher customer purchase intent compared to social media.

The company should improve customer retention and increase revenue by implementing loyalty programs, personalized recommendations, abandoned cart campaigns, and strategies to increase the $106.51 average order value.

The checkout experience should be continuously optimized by simplifying payment steps, offering multiple payment options, and reducing purchase friction to maintain the strong 91.88% payment-to-purchase conversion rate.

The business should use ongoing KPI monitoring, including conversion rates, revenue per visitor, customer journey time, and marketing channel performance, to make data-driven decisions and identify opportunities for sustainable revenue growth.
